Chilli Paneer Recipe

Chili Paneer is a popular Indo-Chinese dish made with crispy cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) tossed in a spicy, tangy sauce. The dish is characterized by its bold, savory flavors and a combination of heat from green chilies, sweetness from soy sauce, and a hint of vinegar. The paneer is typically deep-fried until golden and then stir-fried with a mix of bell peppers, onions, and aromatic spices like ginger, garlic, and soy sauce. It's usually garnished with fresh coriander and served as a starter or appetizer, often paired with steamed rice or noodles. The crunchy texture of the paneer combined with the rich, zesty sauce makes Chili Paneer a favorite in Indian and Chinese fusion cuisine.

Ingredient Required

  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (or as per spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil (for frying and stir-frying)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix the cornflour, all-purpose flour, and a pinch of salt. Add the paneer cubes and toss them gently to coat them with the flour mixture.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the coated paneer cubes until golden brown and crispy. Set them aside on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  3. In the same pan, heat a tablespoon of oil and sauté the ginger-garlic paste for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the slit green chilies, sliced onion, and bell peppers. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes until they are slightly tender but still crisp.
  5. Pour in the soy sauce, chili sauce, vinegar, and sugar. Stir well to combine.
  6. Add the fried paneer cubes into the sauce and toss gently to coat the paneer with the sauce mixture.
  7. Sprinkle the chili powder and garam masala, and mix everything together. Cook for another 2–3 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or noodles.
