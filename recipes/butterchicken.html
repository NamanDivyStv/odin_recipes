Chicken Recipe

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a rich and flavorful Indian dish made with tender pieces of chicken simmered in a creamy tomato-based sauce. The chicken is typically marinated in yogurt and spices, then grilled or cooked until slightly charred before being added to the sauce, which is infused with butter, cream, and aromatic spices like garam masala, cumin, and coriander. Known for its smooth texture and mildly spiced, slightly sweet flavor, butter chicken is often served with naan or basmati rice, making it a beloved comfort food both in India and around the world.

Ingredient Required

  • 500gm chicken
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon chilli powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt.
  2. Add chicken pieces to the marinade and coat well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight).
  3. Grill or pan-fry the marinated chicken until cooked and slightly charred. Set aside.
  4. In a large pan, heat butter and oil over medium heat.
  5. Sauté chopped onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  6. Add tomato puree, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  7. Stir in cream and sugar. Simmer for a few minutes to let the flavors blend.
  8. Add the cooked chicken pieces and mix well. Simmer for another 10–15 minutes on low heat.
  9. Sprinkle crushed kasuri methi and a little butter on top before serving.
  10. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with naan or basmati rice.
